Apprenticeships are work-based training programmes with most of the training being ‘on the job’ – at the employer’s premises. The rest of the training is provided by the College normally on a one day a week basis, although variations can apply according to the programme. Employment is expected to be for a minimum of 30 hours per week which includes the College based element of the training.

As an apprentice you are employed from day one meaning that as you are training and developing your career you will receive a real working wage alongside and be fully prepared for direct employment upon completion of your programme.

- question: Apprenticeships are evolving… id: evolving answer:

Apprenticeships prepare the learner for work through the combination of a qualification as well as real industry working experience. A recent review process has identified a need to make the apprenticeships even more career focused. ‘New Standards Apprenticeships’ will in future support the development of specific skills and knowledge for specific employers and specific job roles. How these are assessed and the qualifications you receive will vary depending on the apprenticeship pathway you choose. Be assured our experienced team of advisors will talk you through what’s involved at every stage.
