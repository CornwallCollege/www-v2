University

There's never been a better time to gain a quality degree right here in Cornwall, with an incredible range of over 80 programmes providing you with real skills to start the career you want straight away. Our programmes are awarded by Plymouth University, one of the Sunday Times' top three modern universities in the UK.

19+

As an adult returning to education you will have many demands on your time. That is why we offer a range of flexible, part-time qualifications. There's something for everyone, from entry-level all the way to postgraduate study.

STEP 1: Decide what course you want to study. You can get advice from either your careers advisor (if you are still at school) or you can book an appointment with an adult guidance officer or a member of our college careers staff.

STEP 2: Come along to one of our Open Days and find out more. This will also give you the opportunity to meet the tutors and discover more about the excellent facilities available at all our campus sites.

STEP 3: Apply online - Search for the course you want to study, decide which option is best for you and click the green APPLY button. Alternatively download a college application form.

If you are still at school you can wait to complete a 'Post 16' form which are normally handed out after the Christmas break, early in the Spring term.

STEP 4: If you are applying for a full-time course, you will be invited to attend an informal interview with a member of the appropriate department. They will assess your suitability and answer any questions you have about the course.

For some courses you may also receive an invitation to attend an Introductory Day (late June/early July) where you can gain some practical experience in your chosen subject(s) and get to know the college a bit better.

STEP 5: Enrol on your course late August/early September. The enrolment dates vary from campus to campus and you will be told your enrolment date well in advance. It's also worth noting that if you are offered a conditional place (based upon gaining certain grades) and you then find your actual grades are lower than expected, or possibly higher, you should contact the College immediately to discuss your options. Are you considering joining us as a full-time* student?

To begin your journey as a full-time university level student with us you will need to apply through Universities and Colleges Admissions Service (UCAS) in the first instance www.ucas.com and then Apply.

Applications are accepted at any time throughout the year subject to availability of places. Upon receipt of an application the Admissions Department will contact you and offer you the opportunity to attend an informal interview, this may be a one to one session, a group session or as part of a taster day, depending on each site. If you are unable to visit the college we will endeavour to make contact via Skype, email or telephone.

When making your UCAS application please ensure that you meet the entry requirements, if you are unsure you can contact the Admissions Department by emailing HEadmissions@cornwall.ac.uk

For information regarding tariff changes for 2017 level 3 applications please click here.

Are you considering joining us as a part-time student?

A UCAS application is not required for part-time study or PGCE/Cert Ed courses. You can apply using the College on-line application form or by contacting the College direct on 0845 223 2567 or email enquiries@cornwall.ac.uk

Student Finance is available for both full and part-time degree level courses. For further information you can go to http://www.gov.uk/studentfinance or http://thestudentroom.co.uk

Some of our campuses hold Open Events, details of which can be found on our website
http://www.cornwall.ac.uk

*Not all of our full-time courses require full-time attendance, they can range from between 1 – 5 days depending on the subject you choose.

Apprenticeship Recruitment Service

Cornwall College, of which Falmouth Marine School is part of, has a dedicated Apprenticeship Recruitment Service specifically designed to match employers with fantastic apprentices. If you are thinking about recruiting an apprentice in any industry or job role, or are looking for an employer to take you on as an apprentice, we will work with you to find the right solution.

If you have found an employer to complete an apprenticeship with, just call us to discuss your training options, enrolment and support with your own dedicated liaison officer, who will ensure you are taken care of throughout your apprenticeship.

Contact us on 01326 310323 or falenquiries@cornwall.ac.uk for more information.
